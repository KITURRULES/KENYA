ABOUT KENYA

CIVIC 05

THE CHARTER

Kenya's Constitution, promulgated in 2010, anchors sovereign power in the people, protects rights and freedoms, establishes national and county government, and separates power across Parliament, the Executive, and the Judiciary.

PUBLIC POWER / PUBLIC DUTY

SOVEREIGNTY

Power belongs to the people

The Constitution frames state authority as delegated power, exercised through national and county institutions.

BILL OF RIGHTS

Rights bind the State

Chapter Four protects civil, political, social, economic, cultural, and group rights.

DEVOLUTION

47 counties matter

County governments bring power and service delivery closer to communities.

CHECKS

Institutions balance power

Parliament, the Executive, Judiciary, commissions, and independent offices form the accountability frame.

BILL OF RIGHTS

FREEDOMS KENYANS CARRY

This is not the full legal text. It is a civic showcase of the rights framework. For legal wording, use the official Constitution text.

DIGNITY

Life, dignity, equality

Protection of life, equal treatment, human dignity, and freedom from discrimination.

LIBERTY

Security and privacy

Freedom and security of the person, privacy, movement, and residence.

VOICE

Expression and media

Freedom of conscience, belief, opinion, expression, media, access to information, association, assembly, and petition.

JUSTICE

Fair process

Fair administrative action, access to justice, fair hearing, and rights of arrested persons.

DEMOCRACY

Political rights

Participation in political choices, voting, representation, and public accountability.

HOME

Property and family

Protection of property, family, marriage, children, older persons, and persons with disabilities.

SOCIAL RIGHTS

Health, housing, food

Economic and social rights include health care, housing, sanitation, food, water, social security, and education.

CULTURE

Language and community

Cultural life, language, consumer protection, labour relations, environment, and group protections.

EXECUTIVE

President, Cabinet, administration

The national Executive implements law, policy, and administration subject to constitutional limits and oversight.

LEGISLATURE

National Assembly and Senate

Parliament represents the people, makes laws, oversees public resources, and protects county interests through the Senate.

JUDICIARY

Courts and independent justice

Courts interpret law, resolve disputes, protect rights, and check unlawful state action.

HOW WE ARE GOVERNED

NATIONAL AND COUNTY POWER

Kenya's political system is a constitutional democracy with elected representation, devolved county governments, independent commissions, and courts empowered to enforce the Constitution.

47 county governments

Devolution creates county executive and assembly structures for local services, planning, budgeting, and representation.

Elections and representation

The electoral system gives citizens a direct path into leadership choices at national and county levels.

Public finance and accountability

Budgets, revenue sharing, procurement, audits, Parliament, county assemblies, and independent offices form the accountability chain.
